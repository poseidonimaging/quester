The Farmers' Children

An exciting book filled with true stories and events in the lives of two Texas families who struggled against the environment, wild animals, and poverty to feed 20 children during the trying times of the Depression.

This book is filled with many true and captivating stories from the lives of these farm children as they struggled to survive the rugged and demanding daily life on the Texas plains.

The Farmer's Sons

A unique book filled with true stories and events in the lives of the sons of a Texas farmer and rancher. The boys were constant companions with their dad in the fields, surviving the severe Texas drought of the 1950s and struggling to provide for the family and livestock during those harsh, drought conditions.

These humorous stories portray the lives of two mischievous brothers at work, play, and school — hunting, fishing and just being boys on a typical Texas farm.

About Quester Publishing

Why the name “Quester”? Webster’s dictionary defines it as an act or instance of seeking, pursuit or search. Therefore, a Quester would be a person who searches or seeks something, thus these publications are for the seeker. Whether you are seeking spiritual truth, interesting facts about farm life, poems, or good clean humor, you will find it here. Not only will you will find these things here, you may even find a book of original Texas country recipes.

Since my youth I have enjoyed telling stories about my life — the interesting and even the strange. I have also enjoyed writing poems. Later in life, after I began to pursue Godly things, I have delighted in sharing little mysteries I’ve discovered in the Bible and have always enjoyed sharing them with others. As a result of these various literary interests, I wanted to share my life with others. I am now seventy years old, and according to most people my age, they want to be able to leave material things behind for their children and grandchildren. But to me, the most important thing anyone can leave to posterity is something from their heart and soul.

How many times have you read a book or an article that inspired you along certain lines? Were you not enriched by the reading? If I can enrich just a few people, then my effort here will have been worthwhile. These things can be shared with millions of people, whereas material things can only be shared with a few.
